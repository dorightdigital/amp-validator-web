Funeral of Salford student found stabbed to death in his flat to take place on Friday

  • by: Neal Keeling
  • 09:45, 18 Mar 2016

William Lound was found at halls of residence in the Blackfriars area of the city on Monday 8 February

The funeral of a University of Salford student who was killed in his flat will take place on Friday morning.

William Lound was found stabbed to death in his halls of residence in the Blackfriars area of the city .

Friends and family of the promising 30-year-old, from Southport, who had been studying IT, will gather at St James’ Church in Birkdale to pay their respects.

William suffered multiple stab wounds in his back and neck during an attack on Monday 8 February.

His body was discovered in his first-floor flat at Bramwell Court, on Cannon Street, in Salford.

At 10.30am William’s coffin will be travel to the church in a horse drawn hearse from his family home in Birkdale, with the rest of the funeral procession following on foot.

After the service he will make his final journey to Duke Street Crematorium ahead of the wake, which will be held at St James Church Hall.

William, who spent two years in the Merchant Navy and also studied at City of Liverpool College, leaves behind his heartbroken sister, business owner Gini, and his mother Mo.

Speaking ahead of the funeral 28-year-old Gini, who will be giving the eulogy, paid tribute to her brother.

Describing him as ‘computer mad’ she said: “William was a fantastic person and amazing friend. He was good at everything.

“He had such a bright future and could have applied his genius to anything. He was obsessed with computers, to the point he would send me to sleep talking

about them.

“He meant such a lot to me.”

  • Lee Arnold, 36, of no fixed address, has been charged with William’s murder.
